7.4.1.6.4.2 Time-domain parameters and mapping from \( i_{\mathrm{t}} \) to time-domain parameters

RIM-RS are transmitted periodically with the RIM-RS transmission period \( P_{t} \) defined in units of the uplink-downlink switching period determined from one or two configured uplink-downlink periods.
- If a single uplink-downlink period is configured for RIM-RS purposes,
- \( \quad P_{\mathrm{t}} \) is the RIM-RS transmission periodicity in terms of uplink-downlink switching periods given by
\[
P_{\mathrm{t}}=\left\lceil\frac{2^{\mu} \bar{P}_{\mathrm{t}} T_{\text {per }, 1}^{\mathrm{RJM}}}{1024 N_{\text {slot }}^{\text {frame }, \mu}}\right\rceil \frac{1024 N_{\text {slot }}^{\text {frame }, \mu}}{2^{\mu} T_{\text {per }, 1}^{\text {RJM }}}
\]
where \( T_{\text {per }, 1}^{\mathrm{RIM}} \in\{0.5,0.625,1,1.25,2,2.5,4,5,10,20\} \mathrm{ms} \);
- \( N_{\text {slot }}^{P t}=2^{\mu} P_{\mathrm{t}} T_{\text {per }, 1}^{\mathrm{RIM}} \) is the total number of slots in a RIM-RS transmission period;
- \( \bar{T}_{\text {offset }}=2^{\mu} i_{\mathrm{t}}^{\mathrm{RIM}} T_{\text {per }, 1}^{\mathrm{RIM}} \) is the slot offset of the uplink-downlink switching period with index \( i_{\mathrm{t}}^{\mathrm{RIM}} \) with respect to the starting boundary of the RIM-RS transmission period
- If two uplink-downlink periods are configured for RIM-RS purposes,
- \( \quad P_{\mathrm{t}} \) is the RIM-RS transmission periodicity in terms of \( P_{\mathrm{t}} / 2 \) pairs of uplink-downlink switching periods and is given by
\[
P_{\mathrm{t}}=\left\lceil\left.\frac{2^{\mu} \bar{P}_{\mathrm{t}}\left(T_{\text {per }, 1}^{\mathrm{RIM}}+T_{\text {per }, 2}^{\mathrm{RIM}}\right) / 2}{1024 N_{\text {slot }}^{\text {frame }, \mu}} \right\rvert\, \frac{1024 N_{\text {slot }}^{\text {frame }, \mu}}{2^{\mu}\left(T_{\text {per }, 1}^{\mathrm{RIM}}+T_{\text {per }, 2}^{\mathrm{RIM}}\right) / 2}\right.
\]
where each pair consists of a first period of \( T_{\text {per }, 1}^{\mathrm{RIM}} \in\{0.5,0.625,1,1.25,2,2.5,3,4,5,10,20\} \mathrm{ms} \) and a second period of \( T_{\text {per }, 2}^{\text {RIM }} \in\{0.5,0.625,1,1.25,2,2.5,3,4,5,10\} \mathrm{ms} \) and where \( T_{\text {per }, 1}^{\text {RIM }}+T_{\text {per }, 2}^{\text {RIM }} \) divides 20 ms ;
- \( N_{\text {sl ot }}^{P t}=2^{\mu} P_{\mathrm{t}}\left(T_{\text {per }, 1}^{\text {RIM }}+T_{\text {per }, 2}^{\text {RIM }}\right) / 2 \) is the total number of slots in a RIM-RS transmission period;
- \( \bar{T}_{\text {offset }}=2^{\mu}\left\lfloor i_{\mathrm{t}}^{\mathrm{RIM}} / 2\right\rfloor\left(T_{\text {per }, 1}^{\mathrm{RIM}}+T_{\text {per }, 2}^{\mathrm{RIM}}\right)+2^{\mu}\left(i_{\mathrm{t}}^{\mathrm{RIM}} \bmod 2\right) T_{\text {per }, 1}^{\mathrm{RIM}} \) is the slot offset of the uplink-downlink switching period with index \( i_{\mathrm{t}}^{\mathrm{RIM}} \) with respect to the starting boundary of the RIM-RS transmission period

The intermediate quantity \( \bar{P}_{\mathrm{t}} \) is given by
\[
\bar{P}_{\mathrm{t}}=\left\{\begin{array}{ll}
\left\lceil\frac{N_{\text {setl } \mathrm{D}}^{\mathrm{RIM}, 1}}{N_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{RIM}} N_{\mathrm{s}}^{\mathrm{RIM}, 1}}\right\rceil R_{1}+\left\lceil\frac{N_{\text {setl } \mathrm{D}}^{\mathrm{RIM}, 2}}{N_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{RIM}} N_{\mathrm{s}}^{\mathrm{RIM}, 2}}\right\rceil & R_{2} \\
\left\lceil\frac{2 N_{\text {setlD }}^{\mathrm{RIM}, 1}}{N_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{RIM}} N_{\mathrm{s}}^{\mathrm{RIM}, 1}}\right\rceil R_{1}+\left\lceil\frac{N_{\text {setl } \mathrm{D}}^{\mathrm{RIM}}}{N_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{RIM}} N_{\mathrm{s}}^{\mathrm{RIM}, 2}}\right\rceil R_{2} & \text { if EnoughIndication is disabled }
\end{array}\right.
\]
where
- \( \quad N_{\text {setlD }}^{\mathrm{RIM} 1} \) and \( N_{\text {setlD }}^{\mathrm{RIM}, 2} \) are the total number of setIDs for RIM-RS type 1 and RIM-RS type 2, respectively;
- \( \quad N_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{RIM}} \in\{1,2,4\} \) is the number of candidate frequency resources configured in the network;
- \( N_{\mathrm{s}}^{\mathrm{RIM}, i} \in\{1,2, \ldots, 8\} \) is the number of candidate sequences assigned for RIM-RS type \( i \in\{1,2\} \) in the network;
- \( R_{1} \) and \( R_{2} \) are the number of consecutive uplink-downlink switching periods for RIM-RS type 1 and RIM-RS type 2 , respectively. If near-far functionality is not configured, \( R_{i} \in\{1,2,4\} \), otherwise \( R_{i} \in\{2,4,8\} \) and the first and second half of the \( R_{i} \) consecutive uplink-downlink switching periods are for near functionality and far functionality, respectively.

The quantity \( N_{\text {symb, ref }}^{\mathrm{RIM}, i} \) is obtained from entry \( \bar{r} \) in a list of configured symbol offsets for RIM-RS \( i \).
7.4.1.6.4.3 Frequency-domain parameters and mapping from \( i_{\mathrm{f}} \) to frequency-domain parameters

The frequency-domain parameter \( k_{1} \) in clause 5.3.3 is the frequency offset relative to a configured reference point for RIM-RS and is obtained from entry \( i_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{RIM}} \) in a list of configured frequency offsets expressed in units of resource blocks.
