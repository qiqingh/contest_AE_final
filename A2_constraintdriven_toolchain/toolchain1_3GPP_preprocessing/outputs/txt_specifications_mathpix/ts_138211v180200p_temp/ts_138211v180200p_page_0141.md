7.4.1.6.3 Mapping to physical resources

The RIM-RS receiver shall assume the reference signal being mapped to physical resources according to
\[
\begin{array}{l}
a_{k}^{(p, \mathrm{RIM})}=\beta_{\mathrm{RJM}} r(k) \\
k=0,1, \ldots, L_{\mathrm{RIM}}-1
\end{array}
\]
where \( \beta_{\mathrm{RIM}} \) is an amplitude scaling factor in order to control the RIM-RS transmission power and \( p \) is the antenna port. Baseband signal generation shall be done according to clause 5.3.3.

The starting position \( l_{0} \) for RIM-RS type \( i \in\{1,2\} \) in slot \( n_{\mathrm{s}, \mathrm{f}}^{\mu} \) in a frame is given by
\[
l_{0}=T_{\text {offset }}^{\mathrm{UD}, \mathrm{RJM}} \bmod N_{\text {symb }}^{\text {slot }}
\]
in slots satisfying
\[
\left(1024 N_{\text {slot }}^{\text {frame }, \mu} \bar{n}_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{RJM}}+N_{\text {slot }}^{\text {frame }, \mu} n_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{RJM}}+n_{\mathrm{s}, \mathrm{f}}^{\mu}-\left(\bar{T}_{\text {offset }}+\left\lfloor T_{\text {offset }}^{\mathrm{UD}, \mathrm{RJM}} / N_{\text {symb }}^{\text {slot }}\right\rfloor\right)\right) \bmod N_{\text {slot }}^{P_{\mathrm{t}}}=0
\]
where
- \( \bar{n}_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{RIM}} \in\left\{0,1, \ldots, N_{\text {slot }}^{P t} /\left(1024 N_{\text {slot }}^{\text {fram }, \mu}\right)-1\right\} \) counts the number of times the SFN periods within the RIM-RS transmission period;
- \( \quad T_{\text {off set }}^{\mathrm{UD}, \mathrm{RIM}}=N_{\text {ref }}^{\mathrm{UD}, \mathrm{RIM}}-N_{\text {symb, ref }}^{\mathrm{RIM}, i} \) where \( N_{\text {ref }}^{\mathrm{UD}, \mathrm{RIM}} \in\{2,3, \ldots, 20 \cdot 2 \cdot 14-1\} \) is the symbol offset of the reference point after the starting boundary of the uplink-downlink switching period in which the RIM-RS is mapped to and \( N_{\text {symb,ref }}^{\mathrm{RIM}, i} \) is obtained as described in clause 7.4.1.6.4.2;
- \( \quad N_{\text {slot }}^{P_{t}} \) is the total number of slots in a RIM-RS transmission period as defined in clause 7.4.1.6.4.2;
- \( \bar{T}_{\text {offset }} \) is the slot offset of the uplink-downlink switching period with index \( i_{t}^{\mathrm{RIM}} \) with respect to the starting boundary of the RIM-RS transmission period and is defined in clause 7.4.1.6.4.2;
- \( \quad P_{\mathrm{t}} \) is the RIM-RS transmission periodicity in units of uplink-downlink switching period as defined in clause 7.4.1.6.4.2.
7.4.1.6.4 RIM-RS configuration
7.4.1.6.4.1 General

A resource for RIM-RS transmission is defined by the indices \( i_{\mathrm{t}}^{\mathrm{RIM}} \in\left\{0,1, \ldots, P_{\mathrm{t}}-1\right\}, i_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{RIM}} \in\left\{0,1, \ldots, N_{\mathrm{f}}^{\mathrm{RIM}}-1\right\} \), and \( i_{\mathrm{s}}^{\mathrm{RIM}} \in\left\{0,1, \ldots, N_{\mathrm{s}}^{\mathrm{RIM}, i}-1\right\} \) used as indices into configured lists of time, frequency, and sequence parameters, respectively.

All RIM-RS resources occupy the same number of resource blocks, \( N_{\mathrm{RB}}^{\mathrm{RIM}} \). At most 32 RIM-RS resources can be configured within a 10 ms period.
