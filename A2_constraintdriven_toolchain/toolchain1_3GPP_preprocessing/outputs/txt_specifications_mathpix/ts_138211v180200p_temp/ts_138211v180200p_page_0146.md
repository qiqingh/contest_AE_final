7.4.2 Synchronization signals
7.4.2.1 Physical-layer cell identities

There are 1008 unique physical-layer cell identities given by
\[
N_{\mathrm{ID}}^{\mathrm{cell}}=3 N_{\mathrm{ID}}^{(\mathrm{l})}+N_{\mathrm{ID}}^{(2)}
\]
where \( N_{\text {ID }}^{(1)} \in\{0,1, \ldots, 335\} \) and \( N_{\text {ID }}^{(2)} \in\{0,1,2\} \).
7.4.2.2 Primary synchronization signal
7.4.2.2.1 Sequence generation

The sequence \( d_{\text {PSS }}(n) \) for the primary synchronization signal is defined by
\[
\begin{aligned}
d_{\mathrm{PSS}}(n) & =1-2 x(m) \\
m & =\left(n+43 N_{\mathrm{ID}}^{(2)}\right) \bmod 127 \\
0 & \leq n<127
\end{aligned}
\]
where
\[
x(i+7)=(x(i+4)+x(i)) \bmod 2
\]
and
\[
\left[\begin{array}{llllll}
x(6) & x(5) & x(4) & x(3) & x(2) & x(1)
\end{array} x(0)\right]=\left[\begin{array}{lllllll}
1 & 1 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 1 & 0
\end{array}\right]
\]
