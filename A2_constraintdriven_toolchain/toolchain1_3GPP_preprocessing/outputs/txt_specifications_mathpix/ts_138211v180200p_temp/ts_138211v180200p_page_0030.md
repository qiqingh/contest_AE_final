5.3.2 OFDM baseband signal generation for PRACH

The time-continuous signal \( s_{l}^{(p, \mu)}(t) \) on antenna port \( p \) for PRACH is defined by
\[
\begin{aligned}
s_{l}^{(p, \mu)}(t)= & \sum_{k=0}^{L_{\mathrm{RA}}-1} a_{k}^{(p, \mathrm{RA})} e^{j 2 \pi\left(k+K k_{1}+k\right) \Delta f_{\mathrm{RA}}\left(t-N_{\mathrm{CP}, l}^{\mathrm{RA}}, T_{\mathrm{c}}-t_{\mathrm{start}}^{\mathrm{RA}}\right)} \\
K= & \Delta f / \Delta f_{\mathrm{RA}} \\
k_{1}= & k_{0}^{\mu}+\left(N_{\mathrm{BWP}, i}^{\mathrm{start}}-N_{\mathrm{grid}}^{\mathrm{start}, \mu}\right) N_{\mathrm{sc}}^{\mathrm{RB}}-N_{\mathrm{grid}}^{\mathrm{size}, \mu} N_{\mathrm{sc}}^{\mathrm{RB}} / 2+n_{\mathrm{RA}}^{\mathrm{start}} N_{\mathrm{sc}}^{\mathrm{RB}} \\
& +\left\{\begin{array}{ll}
n_{\mathrm{RA}} N_{\mathrm{RB}}^{\mathrm{RA}} N_{\mathrm{sc}}^{\mathrm{RB}} & \text { if } L_{\mathrm{RA}} \in\{139,839\} \\
n_{\mathrm{RA}} N_{\mathrm{RB}}^{\mathrm{RA}} N_{\mathrm{sc}}^{\mathrm{RB}} & \text { if } L_{\mathrm{RA}} \in\{571,1151\} \text { in FR2-2 } \\
\left(N_{\mathrm{RB}, \mathrm{UL}, n_{0}+n_{\mathrm{RA}}}^{\mathrm{start}, \mu}-N_{\mathrm{RB}, \mathrm{UL}, n_{0}}^{\mathrm{start}, \mu}\right) N_{\mathrm{sc}}^{\mathrm{RB}} & \text { if } L_{\mathrm{RA}} \in\{571,1151\} \text { in FR1 }
\end{array}\right. \\
k_{0}^{\mu}= & \left(N_{\text {grid }}^{\text {start }, \mu}+N_{\text {grid }}^{\text {size }, \mu} / 2\right) N_{\mathrm{sc}}^{\mathrm{RB}}-\left(N_{\text {grid }}^{\text {start }, \mu_{0}}+N_{\text {grid }}^{\text {size }, \mu_{0}} / 2\right) N_{\mathrm{sc}}^{\mathrm{RB}} 2^{\mu_{0}-\mu}
\end{aligned}
\]
where \( t_{\text {start }}^{\mathrm{RA}} \leq t<t_{\text {start }}^{\mathrm{RA}}+\left(N_{\mathrm{u}}+N_{\mathrm{CP}, l}^{\mathrm{RA}}\right) T_{\mathrm{c}} \) and
- \( \bar{k} \) is given by clause 6.3 .3 ;
- \( \Delta f \) is the subcarrier spacing of the initial uplink bandwidth part during initial access. Otherwise, \( \Delta f \) is the subcarrier spacing of the active uplink bandwidth part;
