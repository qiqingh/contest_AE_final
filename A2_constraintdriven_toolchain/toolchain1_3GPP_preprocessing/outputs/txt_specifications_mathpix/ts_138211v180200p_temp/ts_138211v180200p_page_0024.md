5.2.3 Low-PAPR sequence generation type 2

The low-PAPR sequence \( r_{u, v}^{(\alpha, \delta)}(n) \) is defined by a base sequence \( \bar{r}_{u, v}(n) \) according to
\[
r_{u, v}^{(\alpha, \delta)}(n)=\bar{r}_{u, v}(n), \quad 0 \leq n<M
\]
where \( M=m N_{\mathrm{sc}}^{\mathrm{RB}} / 2^{\delta} \) is the length of the sequence.
Base sequences \( \bar{r}_{u, v}(n) \) are divided into groups, where \( u \in\{0,1, \ldots, 29\} \) is the group number and \( v \) is the base sequence number within the group, such that each group contains one base sequence ( \( v=0 \) ) of length \( M=m N_{\mathrm{sc}}^{\mathrm{RB}} / 2^{\delta} \), \( 1 / 2 \leq m / 2^{\delta} \). The sequence \( \bar{r}_{u, v}(0), \ldots, \bar{r}_{u, v}(M-1) \) is defined by
\[
\begin{aligned}
\bar{r}_{u, v}(n) & =\frac{1}{\sqrt{M}} \sum_{i=0}^{M-1} \tilde{r}_{u, v}(i) e^{-j \frac{2 \pi i n}{M}} \\
n & =0, \ldots, M-1
\end{aligned}
\]
where the definition of \( \tilde{r}_{u, v}(i) \) depends on the sequence length.
5.2.3.1 Sequences of length 30 or larger

For \( M \geq 30 \), the sequence \( \tilde{r}_{u, v}(i) \) is obtained as the complex-valued modulations symbols resulting from \( \pi / 2 \)-BPSK modulation as defined in clause 5.1.1 applied to the binary sequence \( c(i) \) given by clause 5.2.1, initialized with \( c_{\text {init- }} \)
